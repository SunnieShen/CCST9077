**Question1:**  
In quantum mechanics, if a photon is in a superposition of "horizontal polarization" and "vertical polarization", this means:
A. It is a mixture of both horizontal and vertical polarization, and upon measurement, it will collapse into one of them with certain probabilities.  
B. It is actually horizontally polarized, but we just don't know it yet.  
C. It is rapidly switching between the two polarization states.  
D. It is neither horizontal nor vertical, but in an independent state.
**Correct Answer: A**  
**Explanation:**  
Superposition is a fundamental feature of quantum systems. The system is in a linear combination of multiple eigenstates, and only upon measurement does it randomly collapse to a definite state, with probabilities given by the coefficients.

**Question2:**  
Two entangled photons, A and B, are separated to a large distance. When photon A is measured and found to be horizontally polarized, an immediate measurement of photon B will show:
A. Photon B must be vertically polarized (opposite to A).  
B. Photon B's polarization is completely random and independent of A.  
C. Photon B's polarization is the same as A (horizontal).  
D. Photon B's polarization depends on when A was measured.
**Correct Answer: C** (if the entangled state is of the form |HH>+|VV>)  
_Alternatively, a more general correct statement could be:_  
"The polarization of B is strongly correlated with the result of A, and the specific correlation depends on the form of the entangled state."
**Explanation:**  
Entanglement implies that the states of the two particles are inseparable. Measuring one immediately affects the correlated state of the other, regardless of distance.

**Question3:**  
The most appropriate metaphor for "quantum steering" is:
A. Like a remote control, one party can actively control the state of the other.  
B. By choosing the measurement setting, one party can "steer" the state distribution of the other party in a way that cannot be explained by a classical local model.  
C. The two particles are like connected by a string, so that when one moves, the other follows.  
D. One party can send information faster than light to the other.
**Correct Answer: B**  
**Explanation:**  
Quantum steering is a concept that lies between entanglement and Bell nonlocality. It describes the ability of one party, by choosing a measurement setting, to "steer" the state of the other party. This steering effect cannot be simulated by local hidden variable models and reflects the directional nature of quantum correlations

# **Introduction: The Entangled Siblings**

You are a comedic actor, standing in the dimly lit backstage of the Neo-Futurist Theater. Beside you is Alice, a sharp and spirited performer you met only a week ago. Together, you’re here to audition for *Entangled Siblings*, an avant-garde play about twin siblings connected by quantum entanglement. The director insists that only *real twins* can portray the profound, wordless(默契 mòqì, tacit understanding) the script demands.

But you and Alice are not siblings. The air is thick with the scent of old wood and anticipation. You exchange a glance—not of deception, but of determination. You believe true默契 is not inherited; it is built through understanding, collaboration, and shared curiosity.

**Director:** “Welcome. To play the entangled siblings, you must demonstrate a synchrony that transcends the classical.”  
She gestures toward the device. “It will send each of you an ‘emotional prompt’—a colored light. You must respond instantly with a ‘reaction’—a button press.”  
**Director:** “Let’s begin with practice rounds. No penalties. Just learn the rhythm.”

> **Practice Rounds – Rules Explained (Mechanics only, no theory yet):**
> - Emotional Prompt (Light Color):
>   - **Red Light (Prompt A):** Recall a happy memory.
>   - **Blue Light (Prompt B):** Recall a sad memory.
> - Reaction (Button Press):
>   - **Button A:** Smile
>   - **Button B:** Frown
> - Synchronization Rule:
